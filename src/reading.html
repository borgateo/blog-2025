Reading

I don't read as much as I'd like to (life is quite busy) but these are things I keep coming back to.

Sometimes I also cheat a bit with Blinkist. It’s not the same as reading the full thing, but it’s good enough to steal a couple of ideas and decide what’s actually worth my limited attention.

Currently reading

  • Todd Rogers, Jessica Lasky-Fink — Writing for Busy Readers
    A short, practical book about writing for people who are already tired, distracted, and in a hurry. Feels relevant to pretty much every interface and message we ship.
  • L. Frank Baum — The Wonderful Wizard of Oz
    I'm reading it out loud in the evenings for my son. Part bedtime ritual, part rediscovering a classic.

Picked up often

  • Gabriel Weinberg, Lauren McCann — Super Thinking
    I've read it once and now dip back into it from time to time. It's a catalog of mental models — useful when I'm trying to make sense of product decisions and human behavior without overcomplicating things.
  • Marshall Rosenberg — Nonviolent Communication
    One I occasionally come back to. A good reminder that most conflicts are really about unmet needs and poor listening.
  • Dylan Dog (monthly issues)
    My long-term comfort reading. Horror, melancholy and social commentary in comic form. Not "productive", just something that fits my brain when everything else feels too loud.
